  • 2.5 Under the law, content is automatically copyrighted by its creator and becomes their own intellectual property. If content is included from other websites it is always important to ensure you have the legal right to use the material. Failure to comply can result in severe civil and criminal penalties, and is commonly prosecuted to within the full extent of the law.
  • 2.6 Colour blindness can be managed by using a standardised palate, details can be found on the W3C website. High-contrast or monochrome screen layouts can help make screens readable to people who are partially sighted. Screen reader softer can translate text into speech in more drastic situations.
  • 2.7 HTML files contain the markup code for site content. CSS files contain formatting and presentation instructions. JPG files are suitable for graphics containing natural-type images, such as landscapes. PNG files are suitable for graphics containing cartoon-like images, or graphs and charts, typically with lots of primary colours and straight lines or smooth curves. Various other files types are used for audio and video content.
